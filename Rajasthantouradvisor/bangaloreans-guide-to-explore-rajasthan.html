DAY 1 Visiting Jaipur

The busy streets of the city are a riot of color and activity. Cycle rickshaws dodge automobiles, camels, and the occasional lazy elephant as they wind through clouds of soot and pollution. Motorbikes swarm the city's majestic entrances and bustling marketplaces, honking and buzzing. But millennia of history have laid the foundation for the current metropolis of Jaipur. This is the "Land of the Kings," the domain of maharajas, home to more magnificent forts and sumptuous palaces than any other place in India. There are still magnificent remnants of Rajasthan's past to discover. The magnificent City Palace, which still houses the previous royal family, is located in the heart of Jaipur and is an oasis of peace; the magnificent Hawa Mahal, with its honeycomb facade, towers above a congested road.

Following a hearty breakfast, you will only be a short walk from some of Jaipur's main attractions. Of these attractions, the City Palace is undoubtedly the star and with good reason. Situated right in the centre of the old city, you enter through the pink outer walls to find a maze-like complex of gardens, courtyards and buildings. The original outer wall was built by Jai Singh but the interior has been added to and enlarged over the years, with different buildings and palaces deriving from different centuries. DAY 2 EXPLORING THE CITY OF LAKES

City Palace

Day 1 of your trip to Udaipur begins early in the morning at The City Palace, a massive monument and a must-see location in Udaipur. Constructed in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, this palace is one of the biggest in Rajasthan and is situated on a hilltop with a view of the city. In most of India, kings are referred to as Maharajas; but, in Udaipur, Rajput kings are known as Maharanas. Over the course of the next 400 years, the City Palace expanded as 42 different Maharanas left their imprint by constructing additional palaces, or Mahals.
